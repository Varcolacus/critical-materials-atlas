Method · network robustness

Is China’s network centrality just a truncation artifact?

The Network page draws each material’s trade graph from the top-6 partners per side. A smaller graph mechanically concentrates centrality — so does the “China is the broker” finding hold once we stop truncating? Here every 2024 graph is rebuilt at top-6, top-10, top-20, and full (every reporting country), and China’s centrality re-measured at each.

The test. For all 32 materials I rebuild the 2024 directed trade graph straight from BACI at four cuts — top-6, top-10, top-20 nodes and the full uncapped graph — and recompute (i) China’s share of total trade throughput, (ii) its rank by betweenness (is it the single top broker?), and (iii) node-removal fragility. Stable centrality from top-6 to full means the finding is real, not a product of the cap. Computed by build_network_sensitivity.py.
