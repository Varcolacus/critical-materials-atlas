How to read this table

The chain runs reserves → mined → refined → top exporter → top importer: what’s left in the ground (USGS, economically recoverable), who digs it up, who processes it, who sells the most, and who buys the most — the last being the most exposed. Trade figures are world bilateral trade from CEPII BACI. Every country keeps its own colour, so a colour that recurs across a row is one of that supply chain’s main players.

Three marks worth knowing:

  • ⚠ processing chokepoint — the top exporter isn’t the top miner, so a refiner or trader stands in between.
  • ⛓ shared trade code — gallium, germanium and hafnium report under one code and can’t be separated.
  • — no distinct refining stage, or no data.
