What if a key exporter stopped?

For each canonical disruption, the materials whose traded supply is most exposed — and where it would have to re-source. China  ·  Russia  ·  Indonesia  ·  DR Congo  ·  South Africa

Read this as a stress test, not a forecast. "Supply at risk" is the share of world trade the exporter currently supplies — the volume that would need re-sourcing if it stopped. The before→after HHI assumes the remaining exporters keep their current relative shares (first-order reallocation); it is not a price, substitution, or capacity model. ⚠ marks a material with no other exporter of size — where re-sourcing has nowhere obvious to go.
