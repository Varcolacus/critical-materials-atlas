The shock, and its echo

A shock to a producer country doesn’t stop at what it mines. Where a lost material is a host, the by-product companions riding on it fall too — Congo’s copper is also a cobalt shock. This capstone cascades a country’s shock through production, the companion web, and trade, and scores who is the real single point of failure.

How the cascade is computed (and where it stops)

First-order: a shock of S% to a country cuts world supply of each material it mines by S% × its production share (USGS). Companion echo: where a hit material is a host, its by-product companions lose S% × (their by-product reliance on that host) — a second-order hit the first-order view misses. Exposure: the fallout is routed to importing blocs by net trade. The systemic score sums direct hits (weighted up for rigid by-product metals that can’t backfill) plus the echo.

Limits: the echo only fires through hosts the atlas tracks (copper, nickel, bauxite, niobium, platinum, coking coal) — zinc/tin/zircon-hosted companions (germanium, hafnium) are under-counted for want of their host’s production geography. Linear, first-and-second-order only; no price feedback or substitution. A structural map of contagion, not a forecast. Inputs: data.json (production) × companionality.json × net_demand.json → cascade.json.

Where the whole arc lands

Ten layers built one truth in steps: satellites can’t name a mineral → many minerals are by-products → by-products can’t scale → their host’s cycle rules them → demand is surging and nationally concentrated. This capstone puts it in one motion: a shock lands, and it echoes down the companion web into metals the shocked country never even mined, then out to the blocs that buy them. The honest edge remains the same one the atlas has flagged throughout — it is a structural map from public data, not a calibrated forecast; the missing piece is absolute production tonnages and real prices to turn these shares into quantities. That is the next acquisition, and where this atlas would stop being a demonstration and start being an instrument.
