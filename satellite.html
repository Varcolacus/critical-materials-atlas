Method · from orbit

Do the satellites corroborate the supply map?

Every other page measures concentration from trade and production statistics. This one adds an independent physical lens: where mining actually scars the ground, seen from space — 44,929 mapped mine polygons across 145 countries — set beside the atlas’s critical-material producers, with flagship mines snapped to their real satellite footprints. A companion page maps where mining is physically expanding →.

An independent physical check on the supply map: how much mine footprint each country actually shows from orbit, set beside the producers the atlas already names.
Source, and the all-commodity caveat

Source: Maus et al. (2022, Scientific Data) — a peer-reviewed dataset of mine-area polygons from Sentinel-2 imagery (~2019). I aggregate physical mine footprint (km² and site count) per country and lay it beside the production geography the atlas already shows.

Caveat: these polygons are all-commodity (coal, iron, gold and more) with no reliable per-mineral label, so the country map is total mining intensity, not critical-material-specific — the flagship sites below carry the material-specific view.
